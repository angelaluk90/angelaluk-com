Sesame

INDUSTRIAL / UX
PROJECT SUMMARY

Founded in 2013, Atama is a hardware tech startup specialising in designing and manufacturing "Appcessories", physical products that work with your Smartphone or Mac/Computer. From a napkin sketch to final delivery, Sesame was launched in August 2014 with a development time of 6 months. The product is simple; Sesame automatically locks your Mac when you walk away and unlocks it when you returned.

As the sole designer on the team, the role covered all aspects of design, ranging from industrial design of the product from concept to final manufacturable design through to the visual language, flow and final pixels of the accompaning Mac App.

CONCEPT GENERATION
FORM DEVELOPMENT / REFINEMENT

To progress the chosen concept, a series of 3D models and renderings were created to develop the size and form of the product. As the product targets at office workers, Sesame was designed to be worn in various positions, including: on a lanyard, in your pocket or with your keys. The final design had to be light weight and unobtrusive.

In addition, the development process consisted of exploring colour, material and finishes as well as iterating the design to be ready for mass production / injection moulded.

FINAL DESIGN
SESAME MAC APP - CALIBRATION

"It just works"

This was the driving force behind the decisions made for the interaction of the App. We didn't want users to read a manual to set Sesame up; it needed to be obvious. With no interface or visual feedback from the Sesame hardware during use, all interactions are driven through the Mac App.

One of the challenges we faced was the instinctive behaviour of almost all users once they connected the product - Walk as much as possible and see when their Mac would lock. This presented the product with two use cases:

1. Daily normal use

2. Immediate distance testing to check whether their Mac's locked after setup

With Sesame designed and developed to learn your environment and movement overtime, the initial testing users performed (whereby they continually walked to and from the Mac) quickly become problematic for the locking behaviour.

The solution to this was to build a calibration during the setup to begin the environment and movement monitoring as soon as possible. This meant two things, 1. We know the user is at their usual workstation as they are going through the setup so Sesame could begin to monitor their "unlocked" Mac State and 2. By the time users completed the full setup, Sesame would be calibrated to suit their specific environment.

PROJECT SUMMARY

KILO is a body scale which helps you acheive your fitness and health goals. It tracks your weight, BMI, body fat and lean body mass percentages giving you a complete breakdown of how well you are progressing over time. With both an iPhone and iPad App, KILO's eco-system motivates you to keep on target towards your goals.

INITIAL SETUP

There were 3 sections of the setup process that needed to be defined:

1. Connecting App to KILO

3. Setting up user profile

2. Signing up to the Cloud

However, we wanted the setup to be quick and effortless. We didn't want the end user to have to go through a long winded setup process that just to see his/her body weight.

"What's the minimum the user has to do just to stand on the scale and see his/her weight on their iPhone?"

To tackle this, we split the need for each stage of the setup and identified what the consequences would be if we removed that particular setup:

It was decided that instead of forcing the user to setup all sections at the start, the user would only go through the 2nd and 3rd setup phases when required.

The flow began to take shape:

The user would setup the Scale by connecting it to their iPhone App. Once this was complete, they could use KILO and the App to view their weight. This meant that the user would only need to complete their profile or sign up to the Cloud if they wanted to view their detailed breakdown or be able to activate weight tracking.
